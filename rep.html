MindSentences2025 Decoding Project

Project Pipeline & Structure Summary • Last updated: April 2025

🎯 Project Objective

Compare linear decoding (SlidingWindow + RidgeCV) versus deep learning (PiNet) across a predefined linguistic hierarchy for both perception and production conditions, including cross-condition transfer analysis.

📊 Linguistic Hierarchy

The feature extraction process is structured across four escalating levels of linguistic complexity:

🧠 PiNet Architecture Details

ChannelMerger (Spatial Attention)

Learns which MEG channels are most informative dynamically:

  • Input: 306 MEG channels with 2D coordinates.
  • Embeddings: Fourier position embeddings (2048 dim).
  • Compression: Attention-weighted projection down to 270 virtual channels.
  • Regularization: Dropout (0.2).

SimpleConv (Temporal Processing)

Extracts temporal patterns using an expanding receptive field:

  • Layers: 4 dilated convolutional layers (kernel=5, dilation=1, 2, 4, 8).
  • Architecture: Skip connections to preserve fine-grained temporal structure.
  • Output: Global average pooling into a unified feature vector.

Training Specifications

Optimizer: AdamW (lr=1e-3, decay=1e-4) Scheduler: OneCycleLR Early Stopping: Patience=10 Gradient Clipping: 1.0

Subscoring Criteria

Model performance is further evaluated across several behavioral and linguistic dimensions:

  • Lexical Type: Content vs. Non-content words.
    (Content: NN, NNS, VB, VBD, VBG, VBZ, VBP, VBN, JJ, JJR, RB)
  • Sentence Position: is_first_word, is_last_word, and general position_in_sentence.
  • Vocabulary Bounds: Performance on Out of Vocabulary (OOV) tokens.
  • Grammar Breakdown: All 22 individual POS tags.
  • Behavioral Accuracy: Split by Trial Accuracy brackets
    (High >70%, Mid 30-70%, Low <30%).
