Covert Speech MEG Study - Trial Structure

Single Trial Timeline (~8-10 seconds)

1
Fixation Period (Baseline)
1-3 seconds (jittered)
Participant views white fixation cross. MEG records baseline neural activity and monitors for eye movements (EOG), heartbeats (ECG), and head position (HPI coils).
Data recorded: Baseline MEG (306 channels), EOG, ECG, HPI tracking
2
Auditory Stimulus Presentation (PERCEPTION)
1.5-4 seconds (variable)
Sentence audio plays through MEG-compatible earphones at comfortable volume. Participant listens attentively (passive listening task). MEG records auditory-evoked neural responses. Stimulus trigger channel records exact onset time.
Neural signature: Bilateral auditory cortex (STG/MTG), N1m component (~100ms), N400m semantic component (~350-400ms)
3
Post-Stimulus Interval (Processing)
≥2 seconds (minimum)
Audio stops; fixation cross remains visible. Silent period allows cognitive processing of the stimulus. No behavioral task; participant simply waits. This interval is critical for accommodating natural speech rhythm—allows subject to begin inner speech at their own pace without time pressure.
Purpose: Enables individual variation in production latency; prevents forced repetition during pre-planning phase
4
Production Ready Signal
Instantaneous
Fixation cross changes color to green. This visual cue signals to participant: "You may now begin mental repetition whenever you're ready." No forced timing; subject controls when to start.
5
Covert Speech Production (PRODUCTION PHASE)
2-4 seconds (subject-dependent)
Step A: Participant presses first button (START) to mark beginning of mental repetition window. Marks START TIME in MEG data.

Step B: Subject covertly articulates the sentence inside their head at natural, comfortable pace. No overt speech; no visible mouth movement. Purely internal/imagined articulation. Brain areas involved: Motor cortex, premotor cortex, Broca's area.

Step C: When finished with mental repetition, participant presses first button again (END) to mark completion. Duration of button window reflects subject's natural speaking rate for that trial.
Neural signature: Bilateral motor cortex, Broca's area (BA44/45), pre-motor regions. Activity patterns similar to overt speech but with reduced motor artifact.
6
Skip Option (If Needed)
Available at any point
If participant cannot recall or understand the sentence, they can press second button (SKIP) at any time during the trial. Trial is marked "SKIPPED" and automatically moved to end of block for re-attempt later, after all other trials in that run are completed.
Purpose: Maintains task compliance and participant comfort. Typical skip rate: 5-15% of trials. Re-attempted trials usually completed successfully (higher success rate on second try). Skipped trials excluded from analyses if not re-completed.
7
Trial Reset & Next Stimulus
Immediate
Fixation cross returns to white. Next sentence from stimulus set begins playing (returns to step 1). Process repeats until 80 sentences completed per run.

Session & Run Organization

Sessions & Runs

Runs per session:
~5 runs
In-scanner time/session:
~75 minutes
Total session duration:
~2.5 hours (incl. setup, breaks)
Total sessions needed:
~8 sessions per subject
Session spacing:
1-2 weeks apart

Run Constraints

Sentences per run:
80 total
First-3 requirement:
≥3 naturalistic sentences
Remaining 77:
Randomized (natural + controlled)
Run duration:
~15 minutes
Total runs:
40 runs (8 sessions × 5)

Key Design Principles

Variable Button Press Window

Rather than forcing fixed-duration repetition window, subjects press buttons to mark START and END of natural mental articulation. This accommodates individual differences in inner speech tempo and prevents re-repetition during mid-articulation planning.

≥2s Post-Stimulus Interval

Minimum 2-second silence after audio ends allows natural rhythm of inner speech initiation. No time pressure. Prevents subjects from feeling rushed or forced into production before they're cognitively ready.

First-3 Naturalistic Constraint

Every run begins with ≥3 naturalistic (easier, less complex) sentences before controlled stimuli. Prevents cognitive difficulty ramp-up at block initiation. Allows subject acclimation within the scanner environment.

Skip Option Available

If subject cannot recall or understand a sentence, they can skip immediately. Trial re-queued at end of block for second attempt. Maintains compliance, reduces frustration, preserves data quality on re-attempts.

Total Stimulus Yield

Stimuli per subject:
3,200 total (2,000 unique naturalistic + 1,200 shared controlled)
Sessions × runs × sentences:
8 × 5 × 80 = 3,200
Total recording time:
~10 hours per subject (includes buffer time)
Expected usable trials:
~85-95% after artifact rejection (2,720-3,040 trials)
