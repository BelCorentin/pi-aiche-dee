🧠 Global Architecture

Complete Covert Speech MEG Study Pipeline

Overview

This study investigates the neural mechanisms of covert (mental) speech production using MEG. Subjects listen to sentences and then mentally repeat them while neural activity is recorded. Multiple complementary analysis pipelines decode both continuous acoustic tracking (TRF) and discrete linguistic information (Pinet, Ridge) from the MEG signal, enabling direct comparison of perception vs. covert production neural codes.

09 — Critical Constraints & Rationale

≥3 Naturalistic Sentences per Run Start

Naturalistic sentences simpler syntactically than controlled (esp. object-relatives). Starting with simple stimuli prevents cognitive difficulty ramp-up and allows subject acclimation within scanner environment.

Variable Button Press Window

Rather than fixed-duration repetition window, subjects press START/END to mark their natural articulation pacing. Accommodates individual differences and prevents forced re-repetition while mid-planning.

≥2 Second Post-Stimulus Interval

Minimum 2-second silence after audio ends allows natural rhythm of inner speech initiation. No time pressure. Prevents subjects from feeling rushed; accommodates individual latencies.

Skip Option Available

If unable to recall sentence, subjects can skip immediately. Maintains task compliance, reduces frustration. Trial re-queued at block end for second attempt. Typical success on retry: ~80-90%.
