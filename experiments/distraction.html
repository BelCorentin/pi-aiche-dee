Distraction Experiment

Investigating attention mechanisms in challenging environments

Experiment Overview

The Distraction experiment investigates how participants maintain focus during cognitive tasks while exposed to controlled distraction stimuli. We measure neural responses using EEG/MEG to understand the interplay between attention, cognitive control, and distraction resistance.

Methodology

In this experiment, participants performed a primary visual task while being exposed to auditory distractors with varying levels of salience and relevance. Neural activity was recorded using MEG/EEG to capture the temporal dynamics of attention allocation and distraction resistance.

Task Design

The primary task involved identifying target visual stimuli among distractors. The auditory distractions were categorized into:

  • Relevant semantic distractors
  • Irrelevant semantic distractors
  • Non-semantic distractors (pure tones)
  • Control condition (no distractors)
