ExpertLM Experiment

Domain expertise effects on language processing

Home All Experiments

Experiment Overview

The ExpertLM experiment investigates how domain expertise affects language processing and comprehension. By comparing neural responses between expert and novice participants, we can identify how expertise shapes neural representations and processing efficiency.

Methodology

We recorded neural responses (MEG/EEG) from participants with varying levels of domain expertise while they processed both domain-specific and general language stimuli. Experts were defined as having at least 5 years of professional experience in their domain.

Comparison Design

Experts

  • Professional experience > 5 years
  • Advanced domain knowledge
  • Regular engagement with domain terminology

Novices

  • No professional experience in domain
  • Minimal domain knowledge
  • Limited exposure to domain terminology

Studied Domains

  • Medicine
  • Computer Science
  • Law
  • Physics
